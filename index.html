I am a Research Fellow at School of Computing Technologies, RMIT University, under the supervision of Professor Xun Yi. I earned my Ph.D. degree in Computer Science in March 2022. My research pivots on data privacy and security related to machine learning, data mining, cloud computing, and digital health. My current focus is on designing practical secure computation systems for neural network powered applications.

  • Our course Cloud Security (course coordinator Xun Yi) has received top five courses with highest (94.3%) overall course satisfaction (OSI) score at the School of Computing Technologies, RMIT University.
  • Our paper ``MediSC: Towards Secure and Lightweight Deep Learning as a Medical Diagnostic Service'' has received the Best Paper Award of ESORICS 2021.

Research

My current research focuses on privacy-preserving machine learning and deep learning. My goal is to build impactful work that is expected to push forward the deployment of privacy-preserving machine learning on practical usages like medical diagnostics and mobile image classification. My design philosophy is:

  • Devising lightweight and fundamental protocols resort to advanced privacy-enhancing cryptographic techniques for secure computations ranging from data mining to machine learning. I am particularly interested in secure multiparty computation, function secret sharing, (fully) homomorphic encryption.
  • Building secure and practical systems that harness the insights from computer systems, cryptography, machine learning, and data mining. I conduct interdisciplinary research empowering versatile real-world service scenarios, like client-server computation (MediSC, CryptMed), outsourced cloud computation (Sonic, EncSIM), mobile-edge computing (Leia), collaborative computation over distributed data ([ESORICS'19], [TDSC'20]).
